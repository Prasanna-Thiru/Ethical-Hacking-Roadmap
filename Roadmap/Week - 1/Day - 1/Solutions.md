1. What is Ethical Hacking?
Ethical Hacking is the authorized practice of bypassing system security to identify potential data breaches and threats in a network. The person who performs this is called an Ethical Hacker or White Hat Hacker — they do the same things a malicious hacker does, but with legal permission from the owner.

2. Types of Hackers?
| Type | Color Hat | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Ethical Hacker** | ⬜ White Hat | Hacks with permission, finds vulnerabilities legally |
| **Malicious Hacker** | ⬛ Black Hat | Hacks without permission, criminal intent |
| **In-between** | 🩶 Grey Hat | No permission but no malicious intent |
| **Script Kiddie** | — | Uses existing tools without understanding them |
| **Hacktivist** | — | Hacks for political or social causes |
| **State Sponsored** | — | Government-backed hackers |
| **Insider Threat** | — | Attacks from within an organization |
